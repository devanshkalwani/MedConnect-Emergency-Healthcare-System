Emergency First-Aid Guidance

Follow these steps until medical professionals arrive. These actions can help stabilize the situation and save lives.

Cardiac Emergencies
Critical

Heart Attack

  • Call ambulance immediately
  • Keep patient calm and seated
  • Loosen tight clothing
  • Monitor breathing and pulse
  • Do not leave patient alone
Critical

Cardiac Arrest

  • Call emergency services immediately
  • Begin CPR if trained
  • Push hard and fast in chest center
  • Continue until help arrives
Trauma and Injury
Severe

Severe Bleeding

  • Apply firm pressure immediately
  • Use clean cloth or bandage
  • Elevate injured area if possible
  • Do not remove soaked bandage
Severe

Burn Injury

  • Cool burn with running water
  • Do not apply ice
  • Do not break blisters
  • Cover with clean cloth
Neurological Emergencies
Critical

Stroke

  • Check face drooping
  • Check arm weakness
  • Check speech difficulty
  • Call emergency immediately
Moderate

Seizure

  • Do not restrain patient
  • Protect head from injury
  • Turn patient on side
  • Call emergency if prolonged
Respiratory Emergencies
Critical

Choking

  • Encourage coughing
  • Perform Heimlich maneuver if trained
  • Call emergency services
Severe

Breathing Difficulty

  • Help patient sit upright
  • Loosen tight clothing
  • Ensure fresh air access
  • Call emergency services
